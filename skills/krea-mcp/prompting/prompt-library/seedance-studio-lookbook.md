# Studio Lookbook — Seedance

**Use when:** A polished, multi-look lifestyle video with voiceover narration. Person is on screen but in a controlled, styled environment — not a selfie. Product is shown in aspirational context. Think: brand campaign, not UGC.

**Read first:** [seedance.md](seedance.md) for platform rules and the adaptation checklist.

---

## What defines this style

- **Controlled environment:** Clean, styled backgrounds — minimal clutter, intentional props
- **Voiceover narration:** Off-screen voice (or subtle on-screen narration) guides the visual
- **Multiple looks / setups:** 3–4 distinct scenes or angles in 15 seconds
- **Polished but authentic:** Not as raw as UGC, not as sterile as a commercial — editorial lifestyle feel
- **Product is contextual:** The product appears naturally in the setting, not held up to camera

---

## The structure

```
 1. FORMAT HEADER    — duration, format type, mood
 2. TALENT           — appearance, styling, energy
 3. LOOK SEQUENCE    — 3–4 distinct scenes, each with action + setting
 4. VOICEOVER        — what the narrator says across each scene
 5. PRODUCT CONTEXT  — how product appears in each scene
 6. VISUAL LANGUAGE  — lighting, color palette, camera style
```

---

## Layer-by-layer formula

### Layer 1: Format header

```
15 seconds {{FORMAT_TYPE}} video, {{MOOD}}, {{CAMERA_STYLE}}.
```

| Variable | Options |
|----------|---------|
| `FORMAT_TYPE` | lifestyle lookbook, brand campaign, editorial lifestyle video, beauty campaign |
| `MOOD` | warm and aspirational, clean and premium, soft and intimate, bold and confident |
| `CAMERA_STYLE` | handheld editorial, smooth tracking shots, static hero compositions |

---

### Layer 2: Talent

```
A {{AGE_RANGE}} {{GENDER}} with {{APPEARANCE}}, {{SKIN_REALISM}},
wearing {{OUTFIT}} — {{STYLING_NOTES}}.
```

**Skin realism (3–4 cues — always include):**
- `visible pores, slight unevenness in skin tone`
- `minor undereye shadows, hint of shine from natural oils`
- `the kind of skin you see on a real person, not retouched`

**Styling notes for lookbook context:**
- Keep clothing intentional but not costume-y: "a linen blazer over a white tank," "silk slip dress in champagne"
- Match to product's target aesthetic (skincare → clean minimalism; activewear → athletic editorial)

---

### Layer 3: Look sequence

3–4 scenes stitched together. Each scene: **setting + action + how product appears**.

```
[00:00] Scene 1: {{SETTING_1}} — {{ACTION_1}}. {{PRODUCT_PLACEMENT_1}}.
[00:04] Scene 2: {{SETTING_2}} — {{ACTION_2}}. {{PRODUCT_PLACEMENT_2}}.
[00:09] Scene 3: {{SETTING_3}} — {{ACTION_3}}. {{PRODUCT_PLACEMENT_3}}.
[00:13] Final scene: {{CLOSING_SHOT}}.
```

**Scene bank:**

| Scene | Setting | Actions |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Morning ritual** | Bright bathroom, marble counter | Applying product at mirror, linen towel draped over shoulder |
| **Kitchen moment** | Neutral kitchen, morning light | Holding mug + product, looking out window |
| **Bedroom golden hour** | Bed with linen duvet, plants | Sitting on bed reading, product on bedside table |
| **Outdoor terrace** | Clean terrace, greenery | Looking out at view, product on railing |
| **Studio/creative space** | White walls, minimal furniture | At desk or easel, product nearby |

---

### Layer 4: Voiceover

Off-screen narration that guides the visual. Keeps the viewer anchored.

```
Voiceover (warm, measured): "{{LINE_1}}"
{{Scene transition}}
Voiceover continues: "{{LINE_2}}"
{{Scene transition}}
Closing voiceover: "{{LINE_3}}"
```

**Voiceover rules:**
- Warm, measured pace — not salesy
- 1 line per scene (max 8 words per line)
- Present-tense, observational ("She reaches for the bottle." / "A small ritual, every morning.")
- Can be first-person or third-person
- Read aloud at a slow, unhurried pace — if it doesn't fit 15 seconds comfortably, cut words

---

### Layer 5: Product context

Product appears naturally — never held up to camera like a UGC video.

| Context | How it appears |
|---------|--------------|
| **On a surface** | Sitting on bathroom counter, bedside table, kitchen counter |
| **In hand casually** | Held while doing something else, not showcased directly |
| **Mid-use** | Applying or using the product, close-up on hands/skin |
| **Styled prop** | Part of a flat-lay or vanity arrangement |

---

### Layer 6: Visual language

```
The lighting is {{LIGHTING}}. Camera moves are {{CAMERA_STYLE}}.
Color palette: {{PALETTE}}. The feel is {{OVERALL_VIBE}}.
```

**Lighting options:** "soft morning window light," "warm golden hour from the right," "clean overhead studio with soft fill," "candlelight-adjacent warm interior"

**Color palette:** Keep to 2–3 colors: "cream, sage, and natural wood," "white, navy, and gold," "terracotta, sand, and eucalyptus green"

## Example: Skincare brand morning ritual

```
15 seconds lifestyle lookbook video, warm and aspirational, smooth
handheld editorial shots.

A woman in her late 20s with soft brunette hair, medium skin with
visible pores, slight unevenness in skin tone, minor undereye shadows,
wearing a cream linen robe — minimal, clean, morning-ready.

[00:00] Bright marble bathroom — she stands at the mirror, applying
the @(img1) (green glass serum bottle) with fingertips, eyes closed
briefly. Voiceover (warm, soft): "Every morning, the same ritual."

[00:04] Kitchen — she leans on the counter with a white mug, the
serum bottle visible on the shelf behind her. Golden morning light
from the window. Voiceover: "Something small that changes everything."

[00:09] Bedroom — she sits on a linen bed, one hand resting on the
bottle on the nightstand, looking toward the window.
Voiceover: "Your skin, at its best."

[00:13] Close-up of the bottle on the marble counter, steam from a
coffee cup in the background. No voiceover — just the image.

The lighting is soft morning window light from the left. Camera moves
are smooth and unhurried. Color palette: cream, sage, white marble.
No text overlays, no subtitles.
```

## Adaptation checklist

- [ ] 15 seconds, 3–4 scenes with timestamps
- [ ] Talent described with skin realism cues (no retouching language)
- [ ] Each scene has: setting + action + product placement
- [ ] Voiceover is present (1 line per scene, fits runtime when spoken aloud)
- [ ] Product appears contextually, not held up to camera
- [ ] Color palette specified (2–3 colors)
- [ ] Lighting is natural/soft — not dramatic void lighting
- [ ] Word count 100–260 words
- [ ] No forbidden words
